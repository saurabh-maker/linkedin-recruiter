What Top Sourcers Actually Do

"Open to Work" Badge

Green banner visible

That badge often means their network already passed on them. The best people get pulled before they ever need to signal.
Track who's following competitors, liking job posts, or suddenly updating headlines. That's the real "open to work" signal.

3 Years at Company X

Tenure timeline

I've placed "3 year tenure" candidates who were quietly managed out. I've also placed "job hoppers" who left because they saw fraud happening. Tenure means nothing without the story.
Call their skip-level manager or a peer who left 6 months before them. They'll tell you what actually happened.

99+ Endorsements

"Leadership" skills

A CPO I placed had 12 endorsements total. He'd built 3 products to $100M+. The guy with 500 endorsements couldn't ship a feature.
Ignore endorsements completely. Ask: "What shipped because you were in the room?"

3,000 Connections

Network size

Met a CTO with 400 connections who could text any founder in the Valley. Met another with 12K who couldn't get a reference call returned.
Ask: "Who's someone you had conflict with but still respects your work?" That answer tells you everything.

Title: "Senior Director"

Impressive sounding

Some companies hand out Director titles at 28 to retain people. Others make you earn VP over 15 years. Title inflation is out of control.
Ask about budget owned, team size, and reporting line. A "Manager" at Stripe often outranks a "VP" at a 50-person startup.

Profile Looks Complete

Updated info

40% of the best engineers I've placed had profiles that look abandoned. One had a 2015 photo. He'd architected systems handling 2B requests/day.
Patent databases. GitHub. Conference speakers. Academic citations. That's where builders actually leave fingerprints.

Left After "2 Years"

Short tenure flag

What you don't see: their equity cliff was 4 years, and they walked away from $800K because the culture was that bad. That's a candidate with actual standards.
Ask: "What did walking away cost you financially?" Candidates who left money on the table for principles are gold.

Currently at Company X

Employment status

Their CEO just got replaced last week. Their team is being "reorged" next month. They're about to vest $2M and will finally be free. LinkedIn shows none of this.
Set Google alerts for company news. Track leadership changes. Know the vesting schedules. Call at the right moment.

Google, Meta, Amazon

Big tech logos

Half of them won't survive without the infrastructure, processes, and army of support those companies provide. Throw them into a startup and they're lost.
Ask: "Tell me about a time you built something with no playbook and no resources." Watch their face.

Responded to InMail

"Interested" candidate

70% of people who respond enthusiastically use you to get a counteroffer. They never intended to leave. You're free leverage.
First question: "If your current company matches, what happens?" If they hesitate more than 2 seconds, they're not serious.

The Real Game

  • Who answers when someone important calls
  • What people say about them when they're not in the room
  • What they walked away from and why
  • When their handcuffs unlock

The best hire for your role might have 200 connections and a profile picture from 2018.

The worst hire might have 15K followers and a "Top Voice" badge.

Data is the start. Intelligence is the advantage.
